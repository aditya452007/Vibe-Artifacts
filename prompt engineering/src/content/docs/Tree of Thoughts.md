## üåü Introduction
The **Tree of Thoughts (ToT)** is a revolutionary framework for prompting Large Language Models (LLMs). While standard prompting is like a straight line, and **Chain of Thought (CoT)** is like a single path, **Tree of Thoughts** is like a branching map. 

It allows the model to:
1.  **Generate** multiple potential solutions (thoughts).
2.  **Evaluate** each solution for its viability.
3.  **Look Ahead** or **Backtrack** if a path leads to a dead end.

---

## üß† How It Works
Imagine you are solving a complex puzzle. Instead of just guessing the first answer that comes to mind, you:
-   **Step 1: Brainstorming.** You think of three different ways to start.
-   **Step 2: Evaluation.** You realize Method A is too slow, Method B is impossible, and Method C looks promising.
-   **Step 3: Expansion.** You focus on Method C and explore the next steps.
-   **Step 4: Decision.** You reach the final answer by following the best-evaluated path.

### 4. üß© Logic & Reasoning: Complex Riddles
*   **Basic Prompt:** "Solve this: Brothers and sisters I have none, but that man's father is my father's son. Who is in the photo?"
*   **Basic Output:** "His son." (Often correct, but lacks the reasoning steps to prove it).
*   **Tree of Thought Prompt:**
```prompt
Solve the following riddle using a step-by-step Tree of Thoughts process: 'Brothers and sisters I have none, but that man's father is my father's son.'
1. Identify the 'Me' in the sentence and define 'my father's son' based on the constraints.
2. Substitute that definition back into the phrase 'that man's father'.
3. State the final relationship clearly and explain the logic used to verify it.
```
*   **Tree of Thought Output:** A logical proof showing that "my father's son" must be "Me," therefore "that man's father is Me," making the person in the photo the speaker's son.
